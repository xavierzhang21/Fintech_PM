Case Study

Les Escargots Education — Startup Build & Exit

Built a French-learning EdTech startup from zero to 30,000+ users and a successful exit — plus the 'I Do Translation' business sold for ~$400K USD.

OrganizationLes Escargots Education · Beijing
RolePartner & Lead Product Manager
PeriodMay 2015 – July 2019
Impact30K+ Users · 40K+ Followers · $400K USD Exit

Overview

Les Escargots Education was an online education startup specializing in French language learning for Chinese learners — founded and built from scratch during my time in Beijing. Alongside this, I also grew and successfully exited a translation business called I Do Translation, which was sold for approximately $400,000 USD.

Product: The Exam Simulator App

The flagship product was a native mobile app — an exam simulator for French language certification tests. I led the full product lifecycle: ideation, prototyping, development coordination, launch, and iteration. The app grew to 30,000+ active users entirely through product-led growth and community building.

Growth & Marketing

I led the marketing function and built the company's social media presence from zero to 40,000+ followers — coaching the team to adopt agile practices including stand-up meetings, Kanban boards, and regular brainstorm sessions. Revenue grew at 40% annually, driven by a combination of premium app features, exam prep courses, and community membership.

The business continues to operate profitably with a 40% profit margin, serving over 1 million users since inception — long after I exited.

The Translation Exit

The I Do Translation business was built as a complementary arm — leveraging my background as a professional simultaneous interpreter (French/Chinese). I provided interpretation for world leaders including Aung San Suu Kyi, former Canadian PM Jean Chrétien, the Rothschild family, and clients including Intel and Blizzard. The business was successfully sold for approximately $400,000 USD.

Key Learnings

  • Product-led growth in consumer apps requires obsessing over the activation moment — our app's "aha moment" was the first practice test score, not the download
  • A 40% profit margin comes from disciplined prioritization — knowing what to build next is less important than knowing what not to build
  • A successful exit at this scale requires positioning the business as an asset, not just a revenue stream — documentation, processes, and customer data are what buyers pay for
