ScotiaConnect CIAM — Authentication Strategy & $12M Funding

Audited the entire ScotiaConnect authentication landscape, identified critical UX and security gaps, and secured $12M annual funding approval for the CIAM transformation.

OrganizationScotiabank · Global Business Payments
RoleProduct Manager, Digital Channels
Period2022–2024
Impact$12M Annual Funding · 5% → 80% Success Rate

Overview

Authentication is one of the highest-stakes surfaces in digital banking — it's the first thing every user experiences, yet it often receives the least product attention. At Scotiabank, I took on a mandate to audit the entire ScotiaConnect authentication ecosystem and define the path forward.

The Audit

I mapped every authentication flow on the ScotiaConnect platform — login, MFA, session management, device trust, and re-authentication prompts across all major user journeys. The audit identified a significant UX gap between the existing implementation and where customer expectations and competitive benchmarks had moved.

Key findings included:

  • Fragmented authentication experiences across product surfaces, creating customer confusion
  • Legacy authentication methods that increased friction without improving security
  • No unified identity layer, making future feature development slower and riskier

The Strategy

I developed a future-state CIAM architecture that unified identity management across the platform, proposed a phased migration path from legacy systems, and defined the customer experience principles that would guide implementation. The strategy was built to be executable — not just aspirational.

The final presentation secured $12 million in annual funding approval — making it one of the highest-impact strategic deliverables of my time at Scotiabank.

Adobe Analytics — 5% to 80% Success Rate

In parallel, I introduced Adobe Analytics instrumentation to ScotiaConnect for the first time — tracking wire flow performance end-to-end. Initial data revealed a 5% flow success rate, which became the baseline for targeted UX and technical improvements. The second iteration, informed by analytics, lifted that success rate to 80%.

Key Learnings

  • Authentication strategy requires translating deeply technical constraints into customer-experience language for executive audiences — that translation is the PM's job
  • $12M funding came from a presentation that led with customer impact metrics, not architecture diagrams
  • Instrumentation first: you can't improve what you don't measure. The Adobe Analytics initiative proved this in a single release cycle
