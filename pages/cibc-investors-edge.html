CIBC Investor's Edge — Wealth Onboarding Redesign

End-to-end redesign of the self-directed investing onboarding experience to reduce drop-off and accelerate time-to-trade for retail investors.

Organization CIBC
Role Senior Product Manager
Domain Wealth Management / Self-Directed Investing
Type Product · UX Strategy · Onboarding

Overview

CIBC Investor's Edge is one of Canada's leading self-directed investing platforms, serving hundreds of thousands of retail investors. The onboarding experience had grown complex over years of incremental changes — new compliance requirements, regulatory updates, and product additions had created a fragmented, high-friction journey that was causing significant drop-off among prospective investors.

As Senior Product Manager, I led the end-to-end redesign of the account opening and onboarding flow — from initial awareness to first trade.

Problem

The legacy onboarding flow had accumulated significant technical and UX debt. Key pain points included:

  • Multi-day time-to-open due to manual review steps that could be automated
  • High abandonment rates at KYC and suitability questionnaire steps
  • Disconnected experience across web and mobile channels
  • Redundant data collection steps that frustrated users

The core question: how do we meet rigorous regulatory requirements without sacrificing the customer experience that drives conversion?

Approach

I began with a comprehensive discovery phase — interviewing frontline advisors, analyzing drop-off analytics, reviewing competitor benchmarks, and engaging directly with compliance and legal stakeholders to understand non-negotiable requirements.

From this research, I defined a north star metric (time-to-first-trade) and worked with design and engineering partners to create a progressive disclosure framework: surface only the information needed at each step, automate pre-fill where CRA and bank data could be leveraged, and restructure the compliance questions into plain language.

Outcome

The redesigned onboarding flow launched in phases across digital channels. Key results included a meaningful reduction in average time-to-open, improved completion rates through the suitability questionnaire, and positive qualitative feedback from both customers and frontline teams.

Detailed metrics, mockups, and process documentation available upon request under NDA.

Key Learnings

  • Regulatory constraints are design constraints — working with compliance early unlocks creative solutions rather than blocking them
  • Progressive disclosure dramatically reduces cognitive load without removing required information
  • Cross-functional alignment is as important as the product itself — stakeholder buy-in at every stage was critical to delivery velocity
