Change Management Playbook for Financial Services

A repeatable change management framework adopted across a major bank's product organization to improve delivery velocity.

Organization Consulting
Role Management Consultant
Domain Change Management / Organizational Design
Type Strategy · Framework · Change Management

Overview

Large financial institutions often struggle to execute transformation at speed — not because of a lack of strategy, but because of organizational friction: misaligned incentives, unclear decision rights, and change fatigue among delivery teams. This project produced a practical, repeatable change management playbook designed specifically for the realities of regulated financial services organizations.

Context

The engagement was commissioned following a challenging large-scale program delivery where stakeholder misalignment had caused repeated scope changes, timeline slippage, and team frustration. The ask was to diagnose what went wrong and design a framework to prevent recurrence.

Through interviews with program leads, delivery teams, and senior stakeholders, a consistent pattern emerged: changes were being made without a structured impact assessment or communication cascade, leaving delivery teams to absorb ambiguity rather than resolve it upstream.

The Framework

The playbook I developed centered on four principles: early stakeholder mapping, structured change impact assessments at each program milestone, defined escalation paths for scope decisions, and a communication cadence model that matched information density to audience needs.

Each element was designed to be lightweight enough to be genuinely used — not a compliance exercise but a practical tool that made delivery easier, not harder.

Adoption & Impact

  • Adopted across the product organization as standard practice for programs above a defined complexity threshold
  • Measurable improvement in on-time delivery rates in programs that applied the framework
  • Framework iterated based on team feedback — it is now in its third version
