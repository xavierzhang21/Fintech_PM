Strategy

Digital Transformation Roadmap — Retail Banking

A multi-year digital transformation strategy for retail banking operations, aligning stakeholders across technology, compliance, and business.

Organization Scotiabank
Role Management Consultant / Product Manager
Domain Retail Banking / Digital Transformation
Type Strategy · Transformation · Roadmap

Overview

Scotiabank's retail banking division faced pressure from digital-native competitors and shifting customer expectations. I was engaged to develop a multi-year digital transformation roadmap that would define strategic priorities, sequence initiatives by business impact, and establish the governance model needed to execute at scale.

Challenge

The core challenge was not identifying what needed to change — that was well understood. The challenge was sequencing transformation investments to maximize near-term customer impact while building the long-term technical and organizational capabilities required for sustained digital competitiveness.

Stakeholder alignment was particularly complex, spanning technology, risk, compliance, operations, and front-line banking — each with distinct priorities and constraints.

Approach

I led a structured discovery process: stakeholder interviews across 8 business units, competitive benchmarking against Canadian and global peers, analysis of digital channel performance data, and facilitated workshops to build consensus on strategic priorities.

The output was a phased transformation roadmap with clear sequencing logic, initiative interdependencies, resource requirements, and success metrics — built to be actionable at the executive level and operational at the delivery team level.

Impact

  • Roadmap adopted as the foundation for the division's 3-year technology investment plan
  • Governance framework reduced initiative approval cycles significantly
  • Cross-functional alignment workshops became a repeatable model used in subsequent initiatives
