Entrepreneurship: from Start to Exit

Building two EdTech businesses from zero in Beijing — scaling to 1M+ lifetime users and achieving a successful acquisition exit, while building a high-profile professional interpretation practice.

The Ventures

Between 2015 and 2019, I co-founded and led product for two EdTech businesses in Beijing targeting the language learning and international education market — both bootstrapped to profitability with no external funding.

1M+ lifetime users · 30,000+ mobile app users · 40,000+ social media followers · 40% annual revenue growth · 40% profit margin · ~$400K USD acquisition exit

Les Escargots Education

  • Led development of a native app (exam simulator) for French learners, used by 30,000+ users.
  • Grew social media following to 40,000+ by coaching the team on Agile practices — stand-ups, Kanban, regular brainstorm sessions.
  • Achieved 40% annual revenue growth with a 40% profit margin through data-driven backlog grooming and scope validation.

I Do Translation — Acquired for ~$400K USD

  • Built a professional interpretation and translation platform connecting enterprises with vetted multilingual professionals.
  • Scaled to 1M+ lifetime users across the platform ecosystem.
  • Secured high-profile interpretation engagements: Aung San Suu Kyi, Jean Chrétien, the Rothschild family, Intel, and Blizzard.
  • Achieved a successful acquisition exit at approximately $400K USD.

What I Learned

Building with no resources forces ruthless prioritization. Selling directly to customers forces clarity about value. Exiting teaches you that a business is only worth what someone will pay — which depends on how well you've built, documented, and made it transferable.
