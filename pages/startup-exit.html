Case Study

From Zero to Exit — Startup #1

Building and scaling a B2B SaaS product from ideation to acquisition — covering go-to-market, fundraising, and the exit process.

Organization Startup
Role Co-Founder & Product Lead
Domain B2B SaaS / Entrepreneurship
Type Case Study · Startup · Exit Strategy

Overview

Prior to my MBA, I co-founded a B2B SaaS company targeting a workflow inefficiency I had identified firsthand. Over three years, we grew from a founding team of two to a 12-person company, raised seed funding, built a paying customer base, and ultimately exited through an acquisition.

The Idea & Early Validation

The product emerged from a specific pain point in a niche vertical. Rather than building first, we spent the first 3 months in deep customer discovery — 40+ interviews, a no-code prototype, and a waitlist that validated demand before a single line of production code was written.

Early pricing experiments and a pilot with 5 beta customers provided the revenue signal and product direction needed to raise a pre-seed round.

Growth & Product-Market Fit

Finding PMF required several pivots — the initial ICP was too broad, and we needed to narrow our focus to a specific sub-segment where we could win decisively. Once we found that wedge, growth compounded through word-of-mouth and a focused outbound motion.

As product lead, I managed the roadmap, prioritization, and a small engineering team — balancing customer requests against strategic product bets that would expand our addressable market.

The Exit

  • Acquisition by a strategic buyer in our vertical after 3 years
  • Exit provided strong returns for seed investors and the founding team
  • Product and team were retained post-acquisition — the highest form of validation
